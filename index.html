Good Practice Principles in Modelling Defined Contribution Pension Plans

Posted on 2022-08-15 Edited on 2022-09-11

Content

Establish 16 good practice principles for modelling defined contribution(DC) pension plans inluding the following points:

  1. Identify assumption, calibration and projection
  2. The model should specify a risk metric and consequences and deal with uncertainty, member characteristics, charges, longevity risk
  3. Consider both pre- and post-retirement periods outcome risk and allow other sources and factors of income
  4. The model need stress testing, changing with time and scenario and fit for purpose

Method

  1. Fan chart
  2. Value at risk

Goal Based Investment Management

Posted on 2022-08-14 Edited on 2022-08-28

Content

  1. Introduce the dynamic multi-goal optimization framework and solve the stochastic control problem via value function and HJB function
  2. Study the safe level of optimization, the optimal funding ratios and illustrate how the risk profile of investment strategies depends on goal amounts, deadlines and relative priorities
  3. Conduct statics analysis on how the strategies change with respect to goal characteristics and client’s wealth, including goal priorities and distribution of funding ratios

  4. L^2 norm(loss function and regularization)

Optimal consumption and life insurance under shortfall aversion and a drawdown constraint

Posted on 2022-08-13 Edited on 2022-08-25

Content

  1. Establish the objective function of the control problem concerning the shortfall aversion preference on consumption and the expected utility on bequest at the death time
  2. Decompose the domain based on the first order condition and express the piecewise equation piecewisely
  3. Derive some thresholds of the wealth variable for the piecewise optimal feedback controls
  4. Illustrate quantitative properties of the feedback functions by the sensitivity analysis on the force of mortality and drawdown constraint and by observing the change of the optimal wealth, consumption, portfolio, life insurance premium

Points

  1. auxiliary HJB variational inequality
  2. dual transform
  3. piecewise optimal feedback controls

Modern tontine with bequest:Innovation in pooled annuity products

Posted on 2022-08-12 Edited on 2022-08-23

Content

  1. Propose a new tontine structure including a tontine account and a bequest account, whose values are continuously re-balanced to the same proportion
  2. Analyze the similarity and diffence between life annuity, pure tontine and proposed tontine from guarantees and bequest
  3. Establish the model of the value of the two accounts and the utility function of consumption and bequest(power utility and logarithmic utility)
  4. Two interpretations of power utility: a fixed proportion of savings invested in the tontine account and a fixed consumption rate; a fixed proportion of savings invested in the tontine account and a variable consumption rate
  5. Conclusions:as the level of risk aversion increases, the retiree puts a proportion of their pension savings in the tontine account that is fairly constant regardless of the strength of the bequest motive;for retirees with very weak levels of risk aversion, the proportion allocated to the tontine account increases

Points

  1. Makeham’s Law(the force of mortality)
  2. self-financing process
  3. HJB approach
  4. Chini equation

A Multivariate Model of Strategic Asset Allocation with Longevity Risk

Posted on 2022-08-10 Edited on 2022-08-19

Content

  1. Establish reduced-form model for annuity valuation using logarithm
  2. Model the stochastic evolution of ALS(annuity-linked security) returns using a VAR specification
  3. Analyze the impact of longevity secuiritization on optimal allocations including term structure of risk, correlations, generalized absolute minimum-variance(GMV) portfolio allocations, GMV risk, efficient portfolio allocations
  4. Construct the aggregate longevity hedging portfolio(ALHP) by minimizing the variance of the replication portfolio under different constraints
  5. ALHP can be split into a short position in an ALS secuirity and a long position in a portfolio of traded financial secuirities. Use the Sharpe ratio as an actuarial measure of aggregate longevity risk compensation

Methods

  1. vector autoregression model
  2. Lee-Carter model
  3. Sharpe ratio(mean-variance) measure the return on risk

In search of preference shock risks:Evidence from longevity risks and momentum profits

Posted on 2022-08-10 Edited on 2022-08-19

Content

  1. Identify the pricing kernel of agents concerning longevity factor which captures time-preference shocks
  2. Use the Epstein-Zin recursive preferences and rewrite the pricing kernel
  3. Compare the regression results through consumption models, CAPM and Fama-French three-factor model
  4. Use the simple two-factor model and Fama-French three-factor model augmented with the longevity factor to alleviate the multicollinearity problem
  5. Conduct robustness checks including subperiod analysis, alternative test assets, annual estimation, alternative measure of longevity risk: mortality risk
  6. Inspect the economic mechanism from the perspective of equity durations, frequency domain(short-run/long-run), decomposing longevity risk
  7. Conclusion: longevity can affect time preferences directly via the time-preference discount rate or indirectly through the income inequality channel; longevity risk closely tracks the momentum profits; losers have lower dividend growth and hence longer durations than winners.

Methods

  1. log linearization
  2. GMM estimation
  3. CAPM, Fama-French three-factor model
  4. PLC model(extension of the Lee-Carter model)
  5. mimick portfolios
